## Positive: Body-Position Beats

Event-class labels:

- body-position cue
- weak action beat
- posture state

Template ideas:

- `[Name] turned [possessive] head. [Pronoun] crossed [possessive] arms. [Pronoun] blinked and waited.`
- `[Name] blinked. [Pronoun] sat up [modifier]. [Body part] looked/felt [state].`
- `[Name] waited near [place]. [Pronoun] turned [body part]. [Pronoun] crossed [body part] again.`

Examples:

1. Liska turned her head. She crossed her arms over her chest. She blinked twice and waited.
2. Arden blinked. He sat up straighter on the bench. His hands waited in his lap.
3. Cassia turned her head toward the gate. She crossed her paws. She waited without moving.
4. Remal sat up on the crate. He blinked at the empty rope hook. He crossed his thick arms.
5. Mya turned her head from the map. She waited. Her crossed arms stayed tight against her vest.
6. Liska blinked at the chalk line. She sat up. She waited for the others to notice.
7. Cassia crossed her paws on the sill. She turned her head. She blinked when the latch clicked.
8. Arden waited beside the blue bucket. He turned his head toward Cassia. He crossed his arms.
9. Marius blinked once. He sat up in the chair. His crossed arms made the brass badge lift.
10. Cassia waited under the awning. She turned her head. She blinked at the wet street.
11. Liska sat up beside the window. She crossed her arms. She waited while the lantern swung.
12. Remal turned his head toward the stairwell. He blinked slowly. He crossed his arms again.
13. Arden blinked at the red mark. He waited. He sat up so fast the stool scraped.
14. Cassia turned her head from the cart. She crossed her paws. She waited for Remal to speak.
15. Liska waited with one foot on the rail. She blinked. She turned her head toward the whisper.
16. Mya sat up on the roof beam. She crossed her arms. She blinked down at the alley.

- flat action cadence
- adjacent simple sentence
- weak action run
- linking-state run

Template ideas:

- `[Name] [weak action]. [Pronoun] [weak action]. [Possessive body part] [linking state].`
- `[Name] [weak action]. [Pronoun] did not [weak action]. [Pronoun] [weak action]. [Possessive body part] felt [state].`
- `[Name] [weak action]. [Name/pronoun] [weak action]. [Article noun] was [state]. [Pronoun] [weak action].`

Examples:

1. Cassia stood by the gate. She looked at the lock. Her paws felt cold.
2. Arden blinked. He sat on the crate. His face looked tired.
3. Liska turned toward the window. She waited there. Her voice was low.
4. Remal walked to the desk. He stopped beside it. The room was quiet.
5. Mya glanced at the rope. She pulled it once. Her hands felt tight.
6. Cassia stepped into the yard. She did not look back. Her chest felt heavy.
7. Arden crossed his arms. He watched the alley. His shoulders were still.
8. Liska listened at the door. She looked down. Her ears were low.
9. Remal picked up the cup. He set it down. His face seemed dark.
10. Marius stood in the doorway. He looked at Cassia. His voice was quiet.
11. Cassia climbed onto the stool. She rested her paws. The sill was cold.

## No-Hit Controls: Concrete Goal

Control reason: these paragraphs have action, but the action is organized around a concrete task or decision rather than repeated filler beats.

Examples:

1. Cassia crossed the yard to count the blue chalk marks before the guards washed them away. She wrote each number on the crate lid and circled the one mark that did not match the others.
2. Arden walked to the pump because the kettle was empty. He filled it to the black line, carried it back with both hands, and set it beside the stove.
3. Liska looked under the bench for the missing key. She found a bent nail instead, so she used it to lift the latch from the outside.
4. Remal picked up the broken shutter peg and compared it with the fresh splinter in the frame. The peg fit the crack exactly.
5. Mya watched the west gate until the last cart rolled through. Then she closed the ledger and marked the delivery three minutes late.
6. Cassia sat up when the bell rang twice. Two rings meant the north door was open, so she ran there with the spare wedge.
7. Arden moved the bucket beneath the leak and waited until ten drops fell. The count told him the roof patch had failed.
8. Liska rested her paws on the sill to reach the loose tile. She slid the tile aside and pulled out the folded note.

## No-Hit Controls: Cause And Effect

Control reason: these paragraphs connect movement or perception to a cause, consequence, or changed plan.

Examples:

1. The hinge squealed when Cassia opened the hatch, so she wrapped it with cloth before trying again. The second pull made no sound.
2. Arden stopped beside the cart because flour was spilling from the rear seam. He tied the sack shut before the trail reached the guardhouse.
3. Liska glanced at the alley after the lantern went dark. The darkness meant the watch had changed early, so she crossed by the bakery instead.
4. Remal sat up when the map slid off the desk. The fall exposed a second sheet taped beneath it.
5. Mya's tail flicked when dust blew through the cracked wall. She pressed wax into the gap and the draft stopped.
6. Cassia watched the red door because it opened every time the pulley moved. When the rope jerked, she marked the door as the signal point.
7. Arden picked up the brass pin after it rolled from the officer's bag. Its stamped number matched the missing locker tag.
8. Liska waited until the bell finished ringing because the guards counted during the sound. When the yard went quiet, she crossed without being seen.

## No-Hit Controls: Object Change

Control reason: these paragraphs include visible object changes, so the prose is not only camera or body choreography.

Examples:

1. Cassia poured water over the chalk mark. The blue line split into three pale streams and revealed a red mark underneath.
2. Arden tightened the rope around the crate. The loose side lifted flat against the boards.
3. Liska pressed the bent nail into the latch. The latch rose, and the door opened one finger-width.
4. Remal folded the map along the old crease. Two street names lined up and formed a route to the warehouse.
5. Mya scraped mud from the wheel rim. A brass seal appeared beneath it.
6. Cassia set the lantern beside the drain. The light showed fresh scratches inside the iron ring.
7. Arden rubbed ash over the paper. Hidden letters darkened across the blank corner.
8. Liska slid the tile back into place. The roof looked whole again from the yard.

## No-Hit Controls: Measured Physical State

Control reason: these paragraphs use concrete measurements or observable states instead of vague repeated body cues.

Examples:

1. The rope sagged four inches between the hooks. Cassia shortened it by one knot and checked the line again.
2. Arden counted twelve wet pawprints between the gate and the pump. The final print pointed toward the storage room.
3. Liska held the cup under the leak for one minute. It filled to the scratched mark below the rim.
4. Remal pressed the shutter closed and measured the gap with a matchstick. The gap was twice the width of the broken peg.
5. Mya weighed the two sacks by hand, then set them on the market scale. The left sack was half a pound heavier.
6. Cassia marked the candle when the guard entered. By the time he left, the wax had fallen one black stripe.
7. Arden laid the brass pin beside the locker tag. Both numbers ended in 47.
8. Liska counted three cart tracks in the mud. Only the narrow track turned toward the alley.

## No-Hit Controls: Deliberate Early-Reader Pacing

Control reason: these paragraphs use short sentences for clear early-reader pacing, but each sentence advances a concrete sequence.

Examples:

1. Cassia found the red cup. It was under the bench. She put it back on the shelf.
2. Arden saw smoke by the stove. He called Remal. Remal shut the stove door.
3. Liska had one coin. The bread cost two. She chose the apple instead.
4. Mya dropped the key. It slid under the mat. Cassia lifted the mat and found it.

7. Liska opened the small box. A blue ribbon was inside. She tied it around the map.
8. Remal lit the lamp. The room grew bright. Everyone could see the broken latch.
