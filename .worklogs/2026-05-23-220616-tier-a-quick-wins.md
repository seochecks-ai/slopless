# Tier A quick wins from the excellence master plan

Ships the items from `kb/.plans/2026-05-23-205345-slopless-repo-excellence-master.md` Section 9 Tier A that have zero decision surface and no risk of suboptimal positioning.

## In this PR

- **`.editorconfig` at repo root** — UTF-8, LF, 2-space indent, trim trailing whitespace, final newline. Standard defaults; no project-specific judgment.
- **`ROADMAP.md`** — pointer doc that links the promotion master and the (external) excellence master. Satisfies one OpenSSF Silver criterion (`documentation_roadmap`).
- **README "Verify the package" section** — explains the SLSA v1 provenance attestation we now ship and gives the `npm audit signatures slopless` one-liner consumers can run.
- **README star history chart** — single embedded SVG from star-history.com.
- **`help wanted` label applied to issues #17 and #10** — `#17` (template-repetition detector / NLP dep decision) and `#10` (Brysbaert concreteness license) are the only currently-open issues where external help is realistically usable. Other open issues are advanced ML / data-science work that requires deep project context.

## Not in this PR

- **`good first issue` label** — none of the currently-open issues qualify. Most are advanced ML / scorer / dataset work. Labeling those would mislead newcomers. Revisit once we have onboarding-friendly issues (e.g., adding a new pattern rule from a documented dictionary entry).

- **Self-dogfooding (run `slopless` on own docs)** — deferred. Initial run produced 18 findings across 5 files. Most are metric-rule firings (Coleman-Liau / Flesch / Gunning Fog) on technical/boilerplate content: `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md` is verbatim Contributor Covenant 2.1 (cannot change), `SECURITY.md` is standard policy wording, `CONTRIBUTING.md` has legitimately dense technical instructions. Real findings exist (README L77 has fragment-stacking + triple-repeat on the `Exit 0... Exit 1... Exit 2...` line). Wiring this in as a hard CI gate without a per-file rule-disable strategy would either block every PR or require many `textlint-disable` blocks. The calibration decision belongs in its own PR.

- **`npm audit signatures` step in CI, action SHA pinning, SBOM** — re-reviewed in conversation; each has real decision surface (pnpm tooling, first-party-vs-third-party pinning policy, SBOM format/source/tool/failure-handling). Belongs in Tier B once decisions are made.
